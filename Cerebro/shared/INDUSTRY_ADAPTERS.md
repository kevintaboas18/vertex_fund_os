# Industry Adapters

The default formulas are designed for non-financial operating companies. Select an adapter before scoring.

## Banks

- Replace ROIC with ROE, ROTCE, net interest margin, efficiency ratio, CET1, loan-loss reserves, non-performing assets, and tangible-book value.
- Do not use enterprise-value/EBITDA, net-debt/EBITDA, or conventional FCFF.
- Prefer excess-return or dividend-discount valuation.

## Insurers

- Use ROE, combined ratio, reserve development, solvency capital, book-value growth, and excess-return valuation.
- Treat float and investment income explicitly.

## REITs

- Replace EPS with FFO/AFFO.
- Use net debt/EBITDAre, fixed-charge coverage, same-store NOI, occupancy, lease maturities, NAV, and cap-rate valuation.

## SaaS / subscriptions

- Add ARR growth, NRR, GRR, churn, CAC payback, LTV/CAC, RPO, billings, gross-margin profile, and Rule of 40.
- Capitalized sales commissions and stock compensation must be disclosed.

## Biotech / pre-revenue

- Financial score emphasizes cash runway and financing risk.
- Use risk-adjusted NPV by program, phase-transition probability, dilution, patent life, and milestone timing.
- Do not score P/E, FCF yield, or margin quality when not meaningful.

## Commodities and cyclicals

- Normalize price/deck and margins through a cycle.
- Use replacement cost, NAV, reserve life, cost curve, sustaining capex, and mid-cycle cash flow.
- Peak earnings cannot be the base case without a cycle-normalization test.

## Early-stage negative-earnings companies

- Use unit economics, gross-profit growth, cash runway, dilution, and path-to-margin scenarios.
- A DCF is allowed only with explicit scenario probabilities and very low confidence when terminal value dominates.
